Mechanism of Action

Understanding the molecular journey from digestion to cellular utilization.

01. Absorption

Lipid Association

Vitamin A is fat-soluble. It is absorbed with fats in the diet and transported to the liver through the lymphatic system.

02. Storage

Hepatic Reservoir

The liver stores roughly 80-90% of the body's Vitamin A, releasing it into circulation as needed to maintain homeostasis.

03. Transport

RBP Binding

It travels bound to Retinol-Binding Protein (RBP) to reach target tissues without causing toxicity to cell membranes.

04. Utilization

Nuclear Signaling

Inside the cell, it binds to receptors in the nucleus to regulate gene expression for growth and repair.
